American Names Are Becoming Less Regional

At the start of the 20th century, many American names were highly regional. Michaels, for example, were far more common in the Northeast than in the West, after adjusting for population density. But a century of data from the Social Security Administration on Americans' favorite baby names shows that many names have become less regional over time.
